Why Real Food Is the Best Medicine You'll Ever Take

Hey friend,

I've been deep in Dr. Robert Lustig's research this week, and what I found genuinely changed how I think about my plate. Here's the headline: 75% of U.S. healthcare costs come from metabolic diseases that are driven by processed food. Not genetics. Not aging. What we eat.

Lustig, a professor emeritus of pediatrics at UCSF, identified eight cellular pathways that keep our bodies functioning. Processed food—stripped of fiber, loaded with sugar, packed with industrial fats—disrupts every single one of them.

The Key Takeaway: Two rules protect your metabolic health regardless of your dietary philosophy: (1) protect your liver by not overwhelming it with sugar, and (2) feed your gut by eating real fiber. Whether you're vegan, keto, or omnivore, these rules work the same way.

What surprised me most was learning that fructose activates the brain's reward center through the same pathways as cocaine and heroin. Sugar cravings aren't a willpower problem—they're a biochemistry problem. And the food industry knows it.
