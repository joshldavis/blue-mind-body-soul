Health is the new wealth (and the data agrees)

Hey friend,

Here’s a pattern I keep seeing in my coaching work: high-performing people who treat their health like an expense and their bank account like an identity. The trade is always the same — sleep for output, vegetables for convenience, presence for productivity — until the body sends a bill that no salary can cover.

This week, I went deep on the research that quietly demolishes that trade-off. A multi-decade Harvard study found that wealth changes track measurable changes in cardiovascular health — meaning financial stress is, very literally, a biological event. McKinsey calculated that better global health investments could unlock $12 trillion in economic value. Brookings showed that more than half of U.S. counties depend on government transfers because economic opportunity has hollowed out. And the BMJ argued, plainly, that GDP is the wrong scoreboard for a successful society.

This week’s takeaway: You’re not choosing between health and wealth. They’re the same compounding account. Every walk, every full night of sleep, every act of saving, every dinner with someone you love is a deposit. Every shortcut is a withdrawal.

The full article walks through the science from Harvard, McKinsey, Brookings, the WHO, and the BMJ — and lands on five practical moves that build both health and wealth at the same time. It’s the most useful thing I’ve written this month.
