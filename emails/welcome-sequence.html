Hi [Name],

I want to share something simple and practical with you today, because this one thing might have the biggest impact on how you feel.

Eat real food most of the time.

That's it. That's the insight. And it's harder than it sounds, not because we don't know what real food is, but because we've been trained by an entire food system to believe that convenience and optimization matter more than nourishment.

The longevity research is striking: people who live the longest—in the blue zones, in centenarian populations—aren't taking supplements or following complex protocols. They're eating whole foods that grew in their region, prepared simply, shared with people they love.

That's the practice I keep returning to. Not because it's trendy. Because the data is clear.

The Weekly Foods I Return To

Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel): Omega-3s, vitamin D, choline for brain health. I aim for 2-3 servings a week.

Dark leafy greens (spinach, arugula, kale): Packed with polyphenols and minerals. Usually a foundation for lunch or dinner.

Berries (blueberries, raspberries, blackberries): Some of the highest antioxidant density of any food. Frozen is just as good as fresh.

Root vegetables (beets, carrots, sweet potatoes): Prebiotic fiber, minerals, natural sweetness without the blood sugar crash.

Fermented foods (sauerkraut, kimchi, miso): Gut health is longevity health. This is non-negotiable in my kitchen.

Once a week, I get ambitious in the kitchen. Often it's a vegetable-forward smoothie bowl, slow-roasted fish with herbs and olive oil, or a big pot of soup that carries me through several days.

A [AFFILIATE:VITAMIX] changed how I approach food preparation. Being able to blend whole foods (seeds, nuts, leafy greens) into creamy drinks and sauces without the heat that damages micronutrients made cooking feel less like a chore and more like a practice. It's become an extension of how I think about nourishment.

That's the simple shift: invest in a few high-quality tools, choose real food, prepare it with intention, and notice how your body responds. It's slower than optimization. It's also more sustainable, because it's built on pleasure, not restriction.
