3 Standalone Tweets

Most advice about “learning to relax” is psychological. The actual mechanism is physiological: your aorta splits near the navel. Every heartbeat sends a pressure echo. Under stress, it disrupts your circulatory rhythm. Under deep rest, it fades. You relax your body first. Mind follows.
The trick most people miss with meditation: Don't try to quiet your mind. Just stop doing the things that prevent quiet. Phone away. Posture comfortable. Longer exhale than inhale. Alpha and Theta show up on their own once you stop blocking them.
Binaural beats aren't woo. They're a 70-year-old EEG finding dressed up with better audio production. Play tones of slightly different frequencies in each ear — the brain fabricates a phantom beat at the difference, and brainwaves begin to follow it. Headphones required.
