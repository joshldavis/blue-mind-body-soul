Long-Form Post With Discussion Question

Here’s a question I’ve been sitting with this week: when did we start treating health and wealth as separate goals?

The data says they aren’t. A multi-decade Harvard study found that wealth changes — both up and down — track measurable changes in cardiovascular health. McKinsey calculated that better global health investments could unlock around $12 trillion in economic value. Brookings showed that more than half of U.S. counties now depend on government transfers because economic opportunity has hollowed out, leaving older residents stranded. And the BMJ argued, very directly, that GDP is the wrong scoreboard for a successful society.

The takeaway is liberating once you sit with it: every walk, every nutrient-dense meal, every full night of sleep, every act of saving and every act of generosity is a deposit in the same compounding account. You are not choosing between health and wealth. You are building one through the other.
